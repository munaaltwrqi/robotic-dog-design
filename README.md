# 🐕 Robotic Dog Design

## Project Overview

This project presents the conceptual design of a quadruped robotic dog created using Tinkercad. The design focuses on the robot's mechanical structure, leg configuration, stability, and basic walking mechanism.

## Body and Chassis

- Rectangular body structure.
- Symmetrical design for better balance.
- Lightweight frame suitable for a robotic prototype.

## Leg Design

- Four identical legs.
- Each leg consists of an upper and lower segment connected by mechanical joints.

## Degrees of Freedom (DOF)

- 2 Degrees of Freedom (DOF) per leg.
- Total DOF: 8.

## Actuator Selection

Servo motors were selected because they provide precise position control, are lightweight, and are commonly used in robotic applications.

## Preliminary Torque Estimation

A servo motor with an estimated torque of 10–15 kg·cm is suitable for supporting the robot during basic walking.

## Stability and Center of Gravity

The center of gravity is positioned near the middle of the body to improve balance and stability while standing and walking.

## Proposed Walking Gait

The robot uses a crawl gait, where one leg moves at a time while the other three legs remain on the ground to maintain stability.

## Expected Mechanical Challenges

- Maintaining balance during movement.
- Proper weight distribution.
- Joint alignment.
- Servo motor torque limitations.
- Smooth coordination between all four legs.
